01
Cellular Respiration
Glycolysis → Krebs Cycle → ETC → Fermentation → Other Fuels
High-Yield Concepts
The Big Idea: Cells break down glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP) to harvest energy as ATP. It happens in 3 main stages.
MEMORIZE Stage 1: Glycolysis
  • Location: Cytoplasm (no oxygen needed = anaerobic)
  • Input: 1 Glucose (6C) → Output: 2 Pyruvic acid (3C)
  • Uses 2 ATP, makes 4 ATP → Net = 2 ATP
  • Also makes 2 NADH
MEMORIZE Stage 2: Krebs Cycle (Citric Acid / TCA)
  • Location: Mitochondrial matrix
  • Input: Pyruvic acid (broken down, CO₂ released)
  • Per 1 pyruvic acid: 1 ATP, 4 NADH, 1 FADH₂
  • Per glucose (×2): 2 ATP, 8 NADH, 2 FADH₂
MEMORIZE Stage 3: Electron Transport Chain (ETC)
  • Location: Inner mitochondrial membrane
  • NADH → 3 ATP each; FADH₂ → 2 ATP each
  • Protons pumped to intermembrane space → flow back through ATP synthase
  • O₂ = final electron acceptor → forms water

Fermentation (Anaerobic)
UNDERSTAND Why fermentation exists
  • When O₂ is absent, the ETC can't run → NADH piles up → glycolysis stops
  • Fermentation regenerates NAD⁺ so glycolysis can continue → 2 ATP net
MEMORIZE Two Types
  • Alcoholic fermentation: Pyruvate → Ethanol + CO₂ (yeast, bread, wine)
  • Lactic acid fermentation: Pyruvate → Lactic acid, no CO₂ (muscle cells). Brain cells CANNOT do this.
Other Fuel Sources
UNDERSTAND Fats & Proteins as Fuel
  • Fats: Glycerol → glycolysis; Fatty acids → Acetyl-CoA → Krebs. Fats make FAR more ATP than glucose.
  • Proteins: Amino acids stripped of amino group → keto acids → enter Krebs at appropriate step
  • Excess food = stored as fat once carb stores are full

02
Photosynthesis
Light Reactions → Calvin Cycle → Pigments → Photosystems
High-Yield Concepts
The Big Idea: Light energy + CO₂ + H₂O → Glucose + O₂. Essentially the REVERSE of respiration. Happens in chloroplasts in 2 stages.
UNDERSTAND Chloroplast Anatomy
  • Thylakoids: membrane sacs stacked into grana — where light reactions occur
  • Stroma: fluid-filled space around grana — where Calvin cycle occurs
  • Chlorophyll: pigment inside thylakoid membranes — absorbs red & blue light, reflects green
MEMORIZE Pigments
  • Chlorophyll a & b: absorb red + blue → reflect green (why plants are green)
  • Carotenoids (accessory pigments): absorb blue + green → reflect orange/yellow. Hidden in summer, visible in autumn when chlorophyll breaks down.
  • Pigments + chlorophyll organized into photosystems in the thylakoid
MEMORIZE Photosystems I & II (confusingly, II goes FIRST)
  • PSII (first): absorbs light → excited electrons → ETC → pumps protons → ATP made. Splits water → releases O₂ to replace electrons.
  • PSI (second): absorbs light → excited electrons → reduce NADP⁺ to make NADPH
  • Between PSII and PSI: electron transport chain pumps protons → ATP synthase → ATP
Key output of light reactions: ATP + NADPH → sent to stroma for the Calvin cycle.
MEMORIZE Calvin Cycle (Light-Independent / Dark Reactions)
  • Location: Stroma
  • Uses: CO₂ + ATP + NADPH + ribulose (5C sugar, recycled)
  • Enzyme: RuBisCO — combines CO₂ with ribulose (5C) → 6C molecule → splits into two 3C molecules
  • NADPH reduces these → forms G3P (glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate)
  • G3P is the building block for glucose, fats, amino acids, DNA/RNA sugars

03
Cell Division
Cell Cycle → Mitosis → Meiosis → Cancer → Genetic Diversity
High-Yield Concepts
The Big Idea: Cells divide to grow, repair, and reproduce. Two types: Mitosis (1 cell → 2 identical cells) and Meiosis (1 cell → 4 haploid gametes with genetic diversity).
MEMORIZE Cell Cycle & Interphase
  • G1: Cell grows, does its job, decides to divide. Cells that never divide stay here (G₀).
  • S phase: DNA replication. DNA + histones = chromatin. Identical copies connect as sister chromatids at centromere.
  • G2: Final prep — proteins made to move chromosomes.
  • Then: Mitosis → Cytokinesis
MEMORIZE Mitosis — 4 Phases: PMAT
  • Prophase: Chromatin condenses → visible chromosomes. Nuclear membrane breaks down. Nucleolus disappears.
  • Metaphase: Spindle fibers align chromosomes at the equatorial plate (middle).
  • Anaphase: Sister chromatids separate → become daughter chromosomes, pulled to opposite poles.
  • Telophase: Nuclear membranes reform around each set. Chromatin uncoils. Nucleolus returns.
  • Cytokinesis: Animal = cleavage furrow (pinches). Plant = cell plate (builds wall).
MEMORIZE Meiosis vs Mitosis
  • Meiosis has 2 divisions → 4 haploid cells (gametes)
  • Meiosis I = Reduction division: homologous chromosomes separate (diploid → haploid)
  • Meiosis II = like mitosis: sister chromatids separate
  • Key event in Prophase I: Synapsis + Crossing Over — homologs pair up and swap DNA segments → genetic diversity
  • Metaphase I: synapsed pairs (not individual chromosomes) line up at equatorial plate
MEMORIZE Cancer & Checkpoints
  • Proto-oncogenes: encourage cell division. If mutated → oncogenes → cancer
  • Tumor-suppressor genes: discourage division (e.g., p53). If mutated → no brakes → cancer
  • Mutagens = agents that damage DNA. Carcinogens = mutagens that cause cancer (e.g., cigarette smoke → p53 mutation)
  • Benign tumor: doesn't spread. Malignant tumor: spreads = metastasis
UNDERSTAND Genetic Diversity — 5 Sources
  • Mutation, Crossing-over, Segregation, Independent assortment, Fertilization
  • Nondisjunction: chromosomes fail to separate → abnormal gametes → Monosomy (1 copy) or Trisomy (3 copies)
  • Down syndrome: Trisomy 21 = 3 copies of chromosome #21 → 47 total chromosomes

Key Linking Concepts
Ch1 ↔ Ch2: ATP + NADPH are the currencies of BOTH
  • Photosynthesis makes ATP and NADPH in the light reactions → used in Calvin cycle to make glucose
  • Cellular respiration consumes glucose → makes ATP via the ETC using NADH/FADH₂
  • Both use electron transport chains and ATP synthase — the mechanism is the same!
  • Both use proton gradients to drive ATP synthesis (chemiosmosis)
Ch1 ↔ Ch3: ATP powers cell division
  • Cells must be in G1 with sufficient nutrients (ATP available) before committing to divide
  • DNA replication in S phase requires ATP as energy currency
  • Cancer cells have uncontrolled division = require massive ATP → they preferentially use glycolysis even with O₂ present (Warburg effect)
Ch2 ↔ Ch3: G3P from Calvin cycle = raw material for cell division
  • G3P (from photosynthesis) → glucose → glycolysis → ATP for cell division
  • G3P also becomes nucleotides (DNA/RNA building blocks) needed in S phase
  • G3P → amino acids → proteins needed for mitosis spindle fibers
All Three: The shared molecular logic
  • All use enzyme-controlled reactions
  • All require ATP for activation energy
  • Electron carriers (NADH, FADH₂, NADPH) are the shared currency of energy transfer
  • All three processes can be disrupted by mutations → cancer (Ch3) or metabolic disease
The Mirror Relationship
Photosynthesis and Respiration are opposites:
Photosynthesis: CO₂ + H₂O + light → glucose + O₂ (builds up, stores energy)
Respiration: glucose + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + ATP (breaks down, releases energy)
Plants do BOTH. Animals only do respiration.

Multiple Choice — Cellular Respiration
Q1 Where does glycolysis occur?
  • Mitochondrial matrix
  • Cytoplasm
  • Inner mitochondrial membrane
  • Nucleus
✅ Cytoplasm — no mitochondria needed, works without oxygen (anaerobic).
Q2 Tricky! How many NET ATP are produced in glycolysis?
  • 4
  • 6
  • 2
  • 8
✅ 2 NET ATP — 4 are made, but 2 are used to energize glucose first: 4 − 2 = 2.
Q3 What is the role of oxygen in cellular respiration?
  • It splits water to release electrons
  • It powers ATP synthase directly
  • It is reduced in glycolysis
  • It is the final electron acceptor in the ETC, forming water
✅ O₂ accepts electrons at the end of the ETC + combines with protons → H₂O. Without O₂, the ETC stops.
Q4 Tricky! Why can muscle cells survive brief periods without oxygen, but brain cells cannot?
  • Muscle cells can perform lactic acid fermentation; brain cells cannot
  • Muscle cells have more mitochondria
  • Brain cells don't use glucose
  • Muscle cells store more ATP
✅ Muscle cells have lactic acid fermentation enzymes → can make 2 ATP without O₂. Brain cells lack this pathway → die quickly without O₂.
Multiple Choice — Photosynthesis
Q5 Tricky! Which photosystem acts FIRST?
  • Photosystem I
  • Photosystem II
  • Both simultaneously
  • Neither — they are in the Calvin cycle
✅ PSII acts first — despite the numbering. PSII donates electrons to the ETC. PSI receives them later and reduces NADP⁺ to NADPH.
Q6 Where does the Calvin cycle occur?
  • Thylakoid membrane
  • Granum
  • Stroma
  • Cytoplasm
✅ Stroma — the fluid-filled space around the grana in the chloroplast.
Q7 Why are plants green?
  • Chlorophyll absorbs green light
  • Carotenoids reflect green light
  • Chlorophyll absorbs all light equally
  • Chlorophyll reflects green wavelengths (doesn't absorb them)
✅ Chlorophyll absorbs red and blue light but REFLECTS green — so green reaches our eyes.
Multiple Choice — Cell Division
Q8 During which phase do sister chromatids separate in mitosis?
  • Metaphase
  • Anaphase
  • Telophase
  • Prophase
✅ Anaphase — centromeres divide, sister chromatids pulled to opposite poles, now called daughter chromosomes.
Q9 Tricky! What is unique about Prophase I of meiosis vs. Prophase of mitosis?
  • Synapsis and crossing over occur between homologous chromosomes
  • The nuclear membrane reforms
  • Sister chromatids separate
  • Chromosomes become less condensed
✅ Synapsis (homologs pair up) and crossing over (DNA exchange) only happen in Prophase I of meiosis — this creates genetic diversity.

  • Monosomy of chromosome 21
  • A mutation in chromosome 21
  • Trisomy 21 — three copies of chromosome 21

✅ Trisomy 21 = 3 copies of chromosome #21 → 47 total chromosomes instead of 46. Caused by nondisjunction during meiosis.
Short Answer — Reveal When Ready
SA1. Explain why a cell would die if it only had glycolysis and no ETC. How does fermentation help as a temporary solution?
Glycolysis requires NAD⁺ to accept electrons. Without the ETC, NADH cannot be reoxidized to NAD⁺, so glycolysis stops and no ATP is made. Fermentation converts pyruvate to lactate or ethanol, which reoxidizes NADH → regenerates NAD⁺ → allows glycolysis to continue producing 2 ATP. It's a short-term fix only.
SA2. Trace the path of a CO₂ molecule from the atmosphere to becoming part of glucose during photosynthesis.
CO₂ enters the stroma → RuBisCO enzyme grabs CO₂ and combines it with ribulose (5C) → forms a 6C molecule → splits into two 3C molecules → energized by ATP and reduced by NADPH (from light reactions) → forms G3P → G3P used to build glucose.
SA3. How does cancer arise from a molecular standpoint? Include proto-oncogenes and tumor suppressor genes.
Proto-oncogenes code for proteins that encourage cell division (gas pedal). Tumor-suppressor genes code for proteins that discourage division (brakes). When mutagens damage DNA, proto-oncogenes can mutate into oncogenes (permanently stuck ON) or tumor-suppressors can be inactivated (brakes cut). The imbalance leads to uncontrolled division → tumor formation → if malignant, metastasis.
SA4. Compare what happens in Anaphase of mitosis vs. Anaphase I of meiosis.
Mitosis Anaphase: Sister chromatids separate — centromeres divide, pulling identical copies to opposite poles. Cell remains diploid at end.

Meiosis Anaphase I: Homologous chromosomes separate (NOT sister chromatids — they stay together). This is the reduction division — each pole gets one of each homolog, creating haploid sets. Sister chromatids separate later in Anaphase II.

⚡ Cellular Respiration

  • Equation: Glucose + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + 36 ATP
  • Glycolysis: cytoplasm, anaerobic, net 2 ATP + 2 NADH
  • Krebs: mitochondrial matrix, 2 ATP + 8 NADH + 2 FADH₂
  • ETC: inner membrane, NADH→3ATP, FADH₂→2ATP, O₂→H₂O
  • Total: 36 ATP (2+2+32)
  • Fermentation: no O₂ → 2 ATP only. Yeast=alcohol+CO₂. Muscle=lactic acid.
  • Fats: glycerol→glycolysis; fatty acids→Krebs. More ATP than glucose.

☀️ Photosynthesis

  • Equation: CO₂ + H₂O + light → Glucose + O₂
  • Location: chloroplasts (thylakoids + stroma)
  • Chlorophyll: absorbs red+blue, reflects green; a & b forms
  • PSII (first): splits H₂O, releases O₂, makes ATP via ETC
  • PSI (second): makes NADPH
  • Calvin cycle (stroma): CO₂ + ribulose → (RuBisCO) → G3P
  • G3P → glucose, lipids, amino acids, DNA/RNA
  • Carotenoids: orange/yellow pigments, visible in autumn

🔬 Cell Division

  • Interphase: G1 (grow) → S (replicate DNA) → G2 (prep)
  • Mitosis PMAT: Prophase (condense), Meta (align), Ana (separate), Telo (reform)
  • Cytokinesis: animal=furrow; plant=cell plate
  • Meiosis I: reduction division, homologs separate, crossing over in Prophase I
  • Meiosis II: like mitosis, sister chromatids separate → 4 haploid cells
  • Cancer: proto-oncogene (gas) + tumor suppressor (brake) imbalance
  • Trisomy 21 = Down syndrome (47 chromosomes)
  • Diversity: mutation, crossing over, segregation, indep. assortment, fertilization

🔗 The Master Connection

  • Photosynthesis & Respiration are mirror processes sharing ATP synthase + proton gradients + electron carriers (NADH/NADPH/FADH₂)
  • Plants: Photosynthesis makes glucose → Respiration burns it for ATP → ATP powers cell division
  • Animals: Eat plants → Respiration breaks down their glucose → ATP powers cell division
  • G3P (photosynthesis output) → nucleotides → DNA replication in S phase → cell division possible
  • Cancer = cell division dysregulation. Chemo targets fast-dividing cells. Radiation kills tumor cells.

🔥 Must-Memorize Numbers

  • 36 ATP per glucose (aerobic respiration)
  • 2 ATP net from glycolysis
  • 2 ATP from Krebs per glucose
  • NADH → 3 ATP; FADH₂ → 2 ATP
  • 47 chromosomes in Down syndrome (trisomy 21)
  • Diploid (2n) → Haploid (1n) via meiosis
  • 4 haploid cells from meiosis
  • 2 identical cells from mitosis

  • PSII goes FIRST (not PSI)
  • Glycolysis = 2 NET ATP (not 4)
  • Krebs cycle is in the MATRIX (not membrane)
  • ETC is on the INNER membrane (not outer)
